Silk Care Guide

Keep your silk sarees beautiful for generations

Silk sarees are precious heirlooms that can last for decades when cared for properly. Follow these guidelines to maintain the beauty, sheen, and longevity of your Premam Silks sarees.

Washing

  • Dry cleaning is recommended for all silk sarees, especially those with heavy zari work, embroidery, or embellishments.
  • For plain silk sarees without heavy work, you may gently hand wash them using a mild detergent or a silk-specific cleanser in cold water.
  • Never wring or twist the saree. Gently squeeze out excess water and lay it flat on a clean towel to dry.
  • Avoid using bleach, harsh detergents, or fabric softeners on silk.
  • Do not soak silk sarees for extended periods.
  • Always dry silk sarees in the shade. Direct sunlight can fade the colors and weaken the fabric.

Storage

  • Fold sarees with muslin cloth (malmal) placed between the folds to prevent color transfer and creasing.
  • Avoid hanging silk sarees for long periods, as the weight can stretch and distort the fabric.
  • Store sarees in clean cotton bags or cotton pillowcases. Avoid plastic covers, as they trap moisture and can cause mildew.
  • Refold your sarees along different creases every few months to prevent permanent fold lines.
  • Keep sarees in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight and humidity.

Ironing

  • Always iron silk sarees on low heat (silk setting on your iron).
  • Use a pressing cloth (a thin cotton cloth) between the iron and the saree to protect the fabric.
  • Never iron directly on the silk surface, especially on zari or embroidered areas.
  • Iron the saree on the reverse side for best results.
  • Avoid using steam directly on silk, as water spots can form.

Stain Removal

  • Blot, do not rub any spills immediately with a clean, dry cloth. Rubbing can spread the stain and damage the fibers.
  • For minor stains, dab gently with a cloth dampened with cold water and mild soap.
  • For tough stains (oil, turmeric, makeup), take the saree to a professional dry cleaner as soon as possible. Do not attempt home remedies that may damage the silk.
  • Avoid using chemical stain removers or vinegar on silk sarees.

Moth Protection

  • Place dried neem leaves in your saree storage area to naturally repel moths and insects.
  • Camphor (kapur) wrapped in a small cloth can be placed near your sarees to keep pests away.
  • Avoid using mothballs with strong chemicals, as they can leave an unpleasant odor and may damage delicate fabrics.
  • Regularly check stored sarees for any signs of insect damage.

Rainy Season Care

  • Air out your sarees periodically during the monsoon season. Take them out of storage and let them breathe in a well-ventilated, dry room for a few hours.
  • Use silica gel packets in your storage area to absorb excess moisture.
  • If a saree gets damp, dry it immediately in a shaded, well-ventilated area. Do not fold and store a damp saree.
  • Check for musty smells, which can indicate mildew. If detected, air the saree out and consider professional cleaning.

Taking care of your silk sarees ensures they remain as stunning as the day you first draped them. If you have any questions about caring for your sarees, feel free to reach out to us.
